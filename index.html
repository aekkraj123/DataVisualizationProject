Mental Health & COVID-19

About the Project

In February 2020, society throughout the globe saw the rise of a new infectious virus, called the Coronavirus. With the outbreak beginning all the way in Wuhan, China, it was just a matter of weeks before the virus had spread throughout the world, putting what we thought was an end to our social life and how we operate as a society. This then triggered the first global lockdown that we have ever seen. Some months to almost years of being stuck at home, some people had it easy, taking it as a time off to focus on themselves. Others were not too fortunate and had battled new issues faced, that majorly linked to the negative impacts of the economy shutting down.

With that, the goal of this project is to dive deeper into finding out how mental health has changed or developed during the pandemic period. The project’s main goal is to determine if the coronavirus has caused a major impact on mental health issues – to understand if things had gotten worse, better, or remained the same. With the results produced at the end, we should be able to decide as to whether better mental health support should be given out to the public.
